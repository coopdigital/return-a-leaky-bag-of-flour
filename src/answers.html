Accessibility issues in 'Return a leaky bag of flour'

Here is the answer sheet for the 'Return a leaky bag of flour' accessibility testing exercise. There are XX issues in total.

Some of these issues would not be WCAG (Web Content Accessibility Guidelines) 2.1 failures. But, they could present issues to users with access needs, and WCAG compliance doesn't neccessarily mean that something is accessible.

Pages

Contact us

  • Headings are scannable and give context
  • Good colour contrast on text
  • Images are described if needed
  • Links & buttons are descriptive

Can I return my flour?

  • Hit area isn't too small for big fingers
  • Good colour contrast on text
  • Images are described if needed
  • Links & buttons are descriptive
  • Lines lengths aren't too long or too short: 45â€“75 characters per line is acceptable
  • Focus states are clearly visible

Return my flour form

  • Form fields spacing is sufficient
  • Forms use labelled descriptions
  • Tab order makes sense
  • Use HTML not PDFs or Word
  • Interactions (like opening & closing menus) are described in the code

Check answers

  • Document has a language set
  • Follow a logical linear layout
  • There are no keyboard traps
  • Provide another way to do something (e.g contact via phone, letter or email)
  • Use images and diagrams to support text
  • Keep content short clear and simple
  • Write plain English

Confirmation

  • Use subtitles or provide transcripts
  • Landmarks are descriptive
  • The content makes sense zoomed in
  • Animations can be stopped and aren't continuous
